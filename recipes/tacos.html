Bold, Flavorful Mexican Street Food

Tacos are a quintessential Mexican dish made by filling soft or hard tortillas with a variety of ingredients like seasoned meats, seafood, beans, cheese, and fresh vegetables. Tacos are customizable to personal taste, often topped with salsa, guacamole, or sour cream. This handheld dish brings a perfect balance of flavors and textures, making it a popular meal for both casual and festive occasions.

Ingredients

  • Soft or hard taco shells (corn or flour)
  • 1 lb ground beef, chicken, or pork (or vegetarian filling like beans or grilled vegetables)
  • 1 tbsp olive oil
  • 1 onion (chopped)
  • 2 cloves garlic (minced)
  • 1 tsp chili powder
  • 1 tsp cumin
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 1 cup shredded lettuce
  • 1 cup diced tomatoes
  • 1/2 cup shredded cheese (cheddar, Monterey Jack, or your choice)
  • 1/4 cup sour cream
  • Salsa, guacamole, and/or hot sauce (optional)

Instructions

  1. Prepare the filling: Heat olive oil in a skillet over medium heat. Add chopped onion and minced garlic, cooking until softened (about 2-3 minutes). Add the ground meat (or beans/vegetables) and cook until browned. Stir in chili powder, cumin, salt, and pepper, and cook for another 3-4 minutes.
  2. Prepare the toppings: While the meat is cooking, chop the lettuce, tomatoes, and any other desired toppings like avocado or cilantro. Warm the taco shells: Heat the taco shells in the oven (at 350°F for 5-7 minutes) or on a dry skillet over medium heat until warm and slightly crispy.
  3. Warm the taco shells: Heat the taco shells in the oven (at 350°F for 5-7 minutes) or on a dry skillet over medium heat until warm and slightly crispy.
  4. Assemble the tacos: Spoon the cooked meat mixture into each taco shell. Top with shredded lettuce, diced tomatoes, cheese, and any additional toppings like sour cream, salsa, or guacamole.
  5. Serve: Serve the tacos immediately and enjoy!
